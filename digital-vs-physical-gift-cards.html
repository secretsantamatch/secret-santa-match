Digital vs. Physical Gift Cards: Which Should You Give?

The definitive guide to the 2025 gifting debate

It's the modern gifting dilemma. You know you want to give a gift card, but how should you deliver it? Do you text a digital code instantly, or do you mail a plastic card in a greeting card?

The landscape has shifted dramatically. In 2025, 48% of gift cards purchased are digital (e-gifts), up from just 20% five years ago. Yet, 54% of recipients still say they prefer having a physical card to unwrap.

So, which one wins? It depends entirely on who you are buying for and when you are buying it.

The Safety Factor

Etiquette: Is Digital "Lazy"?

Years ago, emailing a gift card was seen as the ultimate sign of laziness. Today, etiquette has evolved.

It is perfectly acceptable to send a digital gift card if:

  • The recipient lives far away (saving you postage).
  • It is for a "just because" or "thank you" moment.
  • The recipient is under 40 (they likely prefer it on their phone).

You should stick to physical cards if:

  • You are attending a wedding or shower where gifts are opened publicly.
  • It is for a major milestone (graduation, retirement).
  • The recipient is older and might struggle to redeem a digital code.
