How to Spot a Gift Card Scam

34% of adults have been targeted by gift card scams. Here is how to stay safe:

üö® RED FLAGS (Do Not Buy)

  • "You owe the IRS money" - Government agencies NEVER ask for gift cards.
  • "50% Off Gift Cards" - If a deal is too good to be true, the cards are likely stolen or empty.
  • Unsecure Website - Never buy from a site without the "HTTPS" padlock icon.
  • Facebook Marketplace - Buying gift cards from strangers is high risk. The balance can be drained remotely after you buy it.

‚úÖ SAFE BUYING PRACTICES

  • Use Trusted Portals: Stick to major retailers or verified marketplaces like Giftcards.com.
  • Check the URL: Ensure it says giftcards.com and not gift-cards-cheap.net.
  • Use Credit Cards: Credit cards offer fraud protection if the site turns out to be fake. Debit cards/Cash do not.

Digital vs. Physical Delivery

When buying online, you usually have two choices:

1. e-Gift Cards (Digital)

  • Speed: Instant (0-5 minutes).
  • Delivery: Email or Text Message.
  • Best For: Last-minute gifts, long-distance friends.
  • Risk: Ensure you type the email correctly!

2. Plastic Cards (Physical)

  • Speed: 2-7 Business Days.
  • Delivery: USPS / FedEx.
  • Best For: In-person parties, weddings, older relatives.
  • Risk: Can be lost in mail (though reputable sites offer replacement guarantees).
