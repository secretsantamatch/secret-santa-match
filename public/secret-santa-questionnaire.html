Why This Questionnaire Works So Well

We designed our questionnaire to be the perfect balance of fun and practical. It's not just a list of "what do you want?"‚Äîit's a guide to understanding someone's personality, which leads to more thoughtful and creative gifts.

Here's What It Covers:

  • Favorites: From colors and snacks to movies and music, this section gives you instant insight into their tastes.
  • Hobbies & Interests: Learn what they do for fun. Are they a gardener, a gamer, a bookworm? This is gift-giving gold.
  • This or That: Quick-fire questions like "Coffee or Tea?" and "Sweet or Salty?" that are fun to answer and super helpful for gifters.
  • "Anything But...": This might be the most important section! It's where people can list allergies, things they already have, or stuff they just don't like. No more guessing games.
  • Wishlist & Sizes: A space for specific ideas and clothing/shoe sizes, just in case.

How to Use the Questionnaire for Your Exchange

Using the questionnaire is super simple and makes you look like the most organized host ever.

  1. Download & Distribute: Send the PDF to everyone in your group via email or your group chat. For in-person exchanges, print out a copy for each person.
  2. Everyone Fills It Out: Give people a deadline to fill out their sheet and return it to you, the organizer.
  3. Draw Names: Use a free Secret Santa generator (like ours!) to draw names. Make sure to set exclusions if you need to!
  4. Share the Info: When you tell each person who their giftee is, attach the completed questionnaire for that person. All the info they need is right there!

Frequently Asked Questions

What questions should you ask on a Secret Santa questionnaire?

A good Secret Santa questionnaire should cover a range of topics to give the gift-giver plenty of ideas. Include questions about hobbies, favorite snacks or drinks, preferred colors, clothing sizes, things they need, things they want, and importantly, things to avoid (like allergies or items they already have too many of).
