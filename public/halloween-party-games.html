15 Free Halloween Party Games for Kids & Adults (No Prep!)

Published: October 2025

Planning a Halloween party can feel like a monster of a task, but coming up with entertainment shouldn't be scary! Forget complicated setups and expensive supplies. We’ve brewed up a list of 15 no-prep Halloween party games that are perfect for kids, adults, and mixed groups. Plus, we have a special treat for you: free printable Halloween bingo cards to make your party an instant hit!

Games for Kids & Families

1. Monster Freeze Dance

How to Play: Put on a spooky playlist (think "Monster Mash"). Have everyone dance like their favorite monster. When the music stops, everyone must freeze in their monster pose. Anyone who moves is out. The last monster dancing wins!

2. Halloween Charades or Pictionary

How to Play: Write down Halloween-themed words (pumpkin, ghost, witch, vampire) on slips of paper and put them in a bowl. Players take turns drawing a slip and either acting it out (charades) or drawing it (Pictionary) for their team to guess.

3. Find the Eyeball (or Pumpkin)

How to Play: A simple Halloween twist on hide-and-seek. Have one person hide a small object, like a plastic eyeball or a mini pumpkin, somewhere in the room. The first person to find it gets to hide it next.

4. Guess the Ghost's Favorite Food

How to Play: Blindfold one player (the "ghost"). Other players feed the ghost different foods from the party snack table. The ghost has to guess what they're eating. It’s hilarious to watch them guess between "witch's finger pretzels" and "gummy worms."

5. Pin the Spider on the Web

How to Play: Draw a large spiderweb on a piece of poster board and hang it on the wall. Give each player a paper spider with a piece of tape on the back. Blindfold them, spin them around, and see who can get their spider closest to the center of the web.

6. Ghost Bowling

How to Play: Decorate a few rolls of toilet paper with black marker to look like ghosts. Stack them in a pyramid and use a small pumpkin as a bowling ball. It’s a silly and fun way to knock over some ghouls!

7. The Mummy Wrap

How to Play: Divide players into teams of two. Give each team a roll of toilet paper. One person is the "mummy," and the other is the "wrapper." The first team to completely wrap their mummy using the whole roll wins!

Games for Adults & Teens

8. Horror Movie Trivia

How to Play: Prepare a list of questions about classic and modern horror films. Ask questions like, "What was the name of the hotel in 'The Shining'?" or "What line is famously misquoted from 'Silence of the Lambs'?" The person or team with the most correct answers wins.

9. Two Truths and a Lie: Spooky Edition

How to Play: Each person tells three "facts" about themselves related to Halloween: two that are true and one that's a lie. (e.g., "I went trick-or-treating until I was 18," "I've seen a ghost," "My favorite candy is candy corn."). The group has to guess which one is the lie.

10. What's in the Box?

How to Play: Fill a shoebox with spooky-feeling items. Think peeled grapes (eyeballs), cold spaghetti (worms), or a wet sponge (brains). Players have to stick their hand in the box without looking and guess what's inside. Their reactions are the best part!

11. Costume Contest with Categories

How to Play: Instead of just one "best costume" winner, create a few fun categories like "Most Creative," "Funniest Costume," "Scariest Costume," and "Best Couple/Group Costume." Let everyone vote by writing down their choices on slips of paper.

12. Halloween Never Have I Ever

How to Play: Players take turns stating something they've never done related to Halloween. For example, "Never have I ever... dressed up as a vampire." Anyone who *has* done it takes a sip of their drink. It's a great icebreaker!

13. Spooky Song Mad Libs

How to Play: Take the lyrics to a famous spooky song like "Thriller" or "Monster Mash." Remove key words (nouns, verbs, adjectives) and ask the group to fill in the blanks without knowing the context. Read the hilarious new version out loud.

14. Halloween Drink "Guess the Ingredient"

How to Play: Create a signature Halloween punch or cocktail. Have guests try to guess the secret ingredients. The person who guesses the most ingredients correctly wins a prize (or bragging rights).

15. Scary Story Circle

How to Play: Dim the lights and have everyone sit in a circle. One person starts a scary story with a single sentence. The next person adds a sentence, and so on. The story can get spooky, weird, or downright hilarious.
