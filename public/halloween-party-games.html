Easy Halloween Games for Kids (Ages 4-12)

Easy Setup

1. Halloween Bingo

Why it works: Kids love bingo, and it keeps them busy while you're setting up other activities. Plus, it's actually entertaining (not just busy work).

What you need: Printed bingo cards, markers (candy corn works great!)

How to play: Call out Halloween items (pumpkins, ghosts, witches). First to get five in a row wins. Simple as that.

Pro tip: Use Halloween candy as markers—kids can eat their game pieces afterward!

Easy Setup

2. Mummy Wrap Race

Why it works: It's hilarious, burns energy, and uses something you already have.

What you need: Toilet paper (lots of it)

How to play: Split kids into teams of 2-3. One person is the "mummy," the others wrap them in toilet paper. First team to use all their TP wins. Pro version: The mummy has to walk across the room without the TP falling off.

Easy Setup

3. Pumpkin Bowling

Why it works: Kids can play it outside or in a garage, and setup is under 5 minutes.

What you need: Small pumpkins, empty plastic bottles or cans

How to play: Set up bottles like bowling pins. Kids roll the pumpkin to knock them down. Keep score like regular bowling.

Easy Setup

4. Pin the Spider on the Web

Why it works: Classic party game with a Halloween twist.

What you need: Poster board, markers, construction paper

How to play: Draw a spider web on poster board. Blindfold kids and have them try to tape a paper spider in the center. Closest wins.

Medium Setup

5. Halloween Scavenger Hunt

Why it works: Keeps kids entertained for 30+ minutes while exploring.

What you need: List of items to find (or hide candy/small toys)

How to play: Hide Halloween-themed items around your house or yard. Give kids a list and let them hunt. First to find everything wins.

Halloween Party Games for Adults (& Teens)

Easy Setup

6. Murder Mystery Party

Why it works: Interactive, engaging, and doesn't feel like a "kiddie" game.

What you need: A free printable murder mystery kit (search "free printable murder mystery" for tons of options)

How to play: Assign each guest a character. They work together to solve a spooky murder mystery throughout the party. Works great as a background activity.

Pro tip: Print character cards ahead of time and give them out as guests arrive. This gets everyone into character from the start!

Easy Setup

7. Halloween Trivia

Why it works: Easy to organize, works for any group size, and people actually enjoy it.

What you need: List of Halloween trivia questions

How to play: Split into teams. Ask questions about horror movies, Halloween history, and pop culture. Most points wins.

Sample questions: "What year was the movie Halloween released?" "What vegetable were jack-o'-lanterns originally carved from?"

Easy Setup

8. Two Truths and a Lie (Halloween Edition)

Why it works: No supplies needed, and it's a great icebreaker.

How to play: Each person shares three "spooky" stories—two true, one fake. Everyone guesses which is the lie. Works great with real-life creepy experiences!

Medium Setup

9. Halloween Would You Rather

Why it works: Conversation starter that leads to hilarious debates.

How to play: Ask spooky "would you rather" questions. Example: "Would you rather be chased by zombies or trapped in a haunted house?"

Easy Setup

10. Costume Contest with Weird Categories

Why it works: Everyone loves an excuse to show off their costume.

Categories to try: Most Creative, Scariest, Funniest, Best Group Costume, Best Last-Minute Costume, Most Likely to Actually Be This IRL

Halloween Party Games for All Ages

Easy Setup

11. Halloween Charades

Why it works: Classic game, Halloween twist. Always entertaining.

How to play: Act out Halloween-related words without speaking. Ideas: zombie, vampire, trick-or-treating, haunted house, carving pumpkins.

Easy Setup

12. Apple Bobbing (The Classic)

Why it works: Timeless, messy, fun. Kids and adults both get competitive.

What you need: Large tub, water, apples

How to play: Float apples in water. Players try to grab one using only their mouth—no hands!

Easy Setup

13. Halloween Pictionary

Why it works: Drawing + guessing = always hilarious.

What you need: Whiteboard or paper, markers

How to play: Teams take turns drawing Halloween words while their team guesses. Set a 60-second timer per round.

Medium Setup

14. Halloween Minute to Win It

Why it works: Fast-paced, competitive, and adaptable.

Game ideas: Stack candy corn, transfer candy with chopsticks, bounce ping pong balls into cups.

Easy Setup

15. Spooky Story Circle

Why it works: Creates atmosphere, no supplies needed, works for any group.

How to play: Sit in a circle with lights off (candles or flashlights only). Each person adds one sentence to create a collaborative scary story. Gets wild fast.

How to Choose the Right Halloween Games for Your Party

Not sure which games to pick? Here's my advice:

For kids-only parties: Choose 3-4 high-energy games (mummy wrap, pumpkin bowling, scavenger hunt) plus one calm game (bingo) for when they need to settle down.
For adult parties: Go with 2-3 games max. Murder mystery works as an all-night activity, trivia is great for teams, and charades is perfect for later when people are more comfortable.
For family parties (mixed ages): Pick games that scale—like bingo, charades, or Halloween Pictionary. Avoid games that require reading or complex instructions.

Last-Minute Halloween Party Tips

Throwing a party this weekend and freaking out? Here's what to prioritize:

  1. Pick 2-3 games max - Halloween bingo + one active game (like mummy wrap) is plenty
  2. Use what you have - Toilet paper, candy, paper, markers. That's enough.
  3. Don't overthink decorations - String lights + a few dollar store items = done
  4. Let music do the work - A good Halloween playlist sets the mood better than decorations
  5. Food matters more than games - If you have to choose, focus on snacks over activities

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the easiest Halloween party games for kids?

The easiest Halloween games for kids are Halloween bingo, mummy wrap race, and pumpkin bowling. All three require minimal setup (under 5 minutes), use supplies you already have, and keep kids entertained for 20-30 minutes.

What Halloween games can adults actually enjoy?

Adults love murder mystery parties, Halloween trivia, and spooky story circles. These games feel sophisticated (not childish) and work well as background activities throughout the party. Skip the games that feel too "elementary school."

How many games should I plan for a 2-hour Halloween party?

Plan for 2-3 games maximum. One calm game (like bingo), one active game (like charades or mummy wrap), and one optional backup game. Parties need time for eating, socializing, and costume admiring—don't over-schedule.

Can you play Halloween party games virtually?

Yes! Halloween trivia, costume contests, and Would You Rather all work great over Zoom. Use breakout rooms for team games and screen sharing for trivia. For charades and Pictionary, the virtual format actually makes them more challenging and fun!

What's the best Halloween game that requires zero preparation?

Two Truths and a Lie (Halloween edition) or spooky story circle. Both need absolutely no supplies, setup, or planning. Just gather people and start playing.

The Bottom Line on Halloween Party Games

You don't need to be a Pinterest mom or spend a ton of money to throw an awesome Halloween party. The best games are the ones that get people laughing, don't require a 20-minute explanation, and use stuff you already own.

My recommendation? Start with Halloween bingo (download it above!), add one high-energy game like mummy wrap or charades, and save a backup game like trivia if the party's going strong and people want more.

Most importantly: relax. Your guests care way more about hanging out, showing off costumes, and eating candy than whether you executed the "perfect" party game.
