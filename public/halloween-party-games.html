10 Hilarious Halloween Party Games for Adults

Updated October 21, 2025 ‚Ä¢ 8 min read

Let's be honest: adult Halloween parties can sometimes fall into a rut. Everyone shows up in costume, stands around with a drink, and makes small talk. It's fine, but it's not *memorable*. If you want to host a party that people actually talk about for weeks, you need some genuinely fun games.

Forget bobbing for apples. We're talking about hilarious, interactive, and slightly competitive games that are perfect for adults. Here are 10 of our favorites.

The Games List

  1. Horror Movie Trivia

    Create a list of questions ranging from easy ("What's the killer's name in 'Halloween'?") to expert-level ("What brand of chainsaw does Leatherface use?"). Divide into teams and watch the horror buffs shine.

  2. Pumpkin Pong

    It's beer pong, but with mini pumpkins or plastic cauldrons instead of red cups. Line them up on a table and have teams try to sink ping pong balls. Simple, effective, and always a hit.

  3. The Mummy Wrap Race

    A classic for a reason. Split into teams of two. One person is the "mummy," the other is the wrapper. The first team to completely wrap their mummy using a full roll of toilet paper wins. It's chaotic and hilarious.

  4. Guess the Villain

    Write the names of famous horror movie villains on sticky notes. Without looking, each guest sticks one to their forehead. They then have to ask yes/no questions to the group to guess which villain they are.

  5. Halloween Charades or Pictionary

    Create a bowl of Halloween-themed prompts: "Carving a pumpkin," "A vampire brushing his teeth," "Trying to run away in a dream." The results are always entertaining.

  1. Two Rooms and a Boom (Spooky Edition)

    This is a fantastic social deduction game for larger groups. Secretly assign roles like "Vampire Hunter" and "Head Vampire." The hunters must figure out who the vampire is before the "timer" runs out. There are tons of free printable versions online.

  2. The 'What's in the Box?' Challenge

    Get a few shoeboxes and cut a hole in the side. Fill them with spooky-feeling items: cold, cooked spaghetti ("worms"), peeled grapes ("eyeballs"), a latex glove filled with flour ("a ghostly hand"). Guests have to guess what's inside just by feeling.

  3. Costume Awards Ceremony

    Instead of just "Best Costume," create funny and specific categories: "Most Likely to Actually Scare Someone," "Best Pun," "Most Creative Use of Cardboard," "Best Couple/Group Costume." Let everyone vote.

  4. Murder Mystery Game

    This requires more prep but is incredibly rewarding. You can buy pre-made murder mystery kits online. Assign characters to your guests beforehand and let the drama unfold throughout the night.

  5. Halloween-themed "Never Have I Ever"

    Give everyone a pile of candy corn. Go around the circle with Halloween-themed prompts: "Never have I ever... screamed during a horror movie," "...eaten all my kids' good candy," "...worn a costume to work when no one else did." If you've done it, you eat a piece of candy.
