The Problem with "More is More"

Elfster operates like a social network for gift-giving. It's designed to keep you on their platform. This means:

  • Mandatory Sign-Ups: Everyone in your group, from tech-savvy coworkers to your grandma, has to create an Elfster account.
  • Data Collection: It requires names and emails, and its business model relies on tracking your shopping habits via its universal wishlist.
  • Complexity: With so many features, the simple act of drawing names can get lost in a sea of notifications, messages, and settings.
  • App Fatigue: Do your friends really want to download another app and remember another password just for a one-time event?

For a simple, private gift exchange with family or a small office, this is often too much friction.

The Simple Alternative: A Privacy-First Generator

Tools like SecretSantaMatch.com are built on a different philosophy: give the organizer the power and get out of the way. There are no accounts, no required emails, and no platform to join. You generate unique, private links for each person and share them yourself. It's a tool, not a social network.
