The Core Problem: Forced Sign-Ups for Everyone

  • Privacy Issues: Everyone has to provide personal data (name, email) just to participate in a simple game.
  • Participation Drop-off: You'll always have a few people who forget their password, can't be bothered to sign up, or whose invitation goes to spam.
  • Complexity: Wishlists, public questions, thank-you notes... it's a lot to navigate for a one-off event.

A modern alternative like SecretSantaMatch.com is built on a "tool, not a platform" philosophy. It's designed to be used once, perfectly, and then get out of your way.

The "Tool, Not a Platform" Approach:

  1. You, the organizer, visit a single webpage.
  2. You enter names, set rules, and style your cards.
  3. The tool generates a unique, private link for each person.
  4. You copy and paste these links directly to your participants via text, email, or group chat.

There are no accounts, no passwords, no data collection. It's the digital equivalent of drawing names from a hat, but with all the modern features you actually need.
