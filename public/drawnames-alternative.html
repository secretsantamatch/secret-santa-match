The Big Difference: Data Collection

DrawNames works by collecting email addresses for every participant. The site then sends the matches directly to each person's inbox. This model has a few problems:

  • Privacy Concerns: You're handing over a list of your friends' and family's emails to a third party.
  • Spam Filters: Important match emails can get lost in spam or junk folders, causing confusion.
  • Typos: One wrong letter in an email address and someone is left out of the game.
  • Extra Steps: Participants often have to create an account or confirm their email, adding friction to a process that should be simple.

A modern alternative like SecretSantaMatch.com flips this model on its head. Instead of collecting data, it gives control back to you, the organizer.

The No-Email Approach (How It Works):

  1. You enter the names of your participants.
  2. The generator creates a unique, private link for each person.
  3. You, the organizer, copy and paste these links into a text message, a group chat, or a private email.

That's it. Your participants' data is never stored on a server, and you control exactly how and when they get their match. It’s faster, more secure, and respects everyone's privacy.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best free Secret Santa generator?

The "best" generator depends on your priorities. If you value privacy and speed, a no-email-required tool like SecretSantaMatch.com is the best choice. If you prefer a more managed experience and don't mind sharing emails, DrawNames is a popular option.

How can I do a Secret Santa with remote participants?

This is where online generators shine! Using a link-based system is perfect for remote groups. Simply generate the matches and send each person their private link via your group chat or individual messages. You can even include shipping addresses in the "Notes" section for each participant.
