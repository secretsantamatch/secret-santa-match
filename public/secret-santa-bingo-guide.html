How to Make Your Secret Santa Party Actually Fun

Updated October 28, 2025 • 10 min read

Let's be honest: the gift exchange part of a Secret Santa party can sometimes be... awkward. People open gifts one by one, say a polite "thank you," and then there's a lull. What if you could turn that moment into the most fun part of the night? Enter Secret Santa Bingo.

This isn't your grandma's bingo. It's a hilarious, interactive icebreaker that gets everyone talking, laughing, and paying attention to every single gift. We've created the ultimate guide to playing, complete with free printable bingo cards and a full PDF guide to make you look like a party-planning genius.

2. How to Play: The Rules

The Goal

Get 5 squares in a row—horizontal (↔), vertical (↕), or diagonal ( diagonale ). The first person to do it yells "BINGO!" and wins.

How It Works

  • Each person gets a unique 5x5 bingo card with 25 squares.
  • Each square describes a person (e.g., 'Has kids under 10' or 'Loves spicy food').
  • Walk around and find people who match each description.
  • When you find someone, mark that square off.
  • The center square is a FREE space (already marked).

Important Rules

  • You CAN ask the same person for multiple squares.
  • You CAN mark a square if YOU match the description.
  • People can self-identify (raise their hand, shout "That's me!").
  • DON'T lie to get squares (keep it honest and fun!).

3. For Organizers: Setup, Tips & Tricks

Before the Party

  • Print Cards: Print all 8 pages of the bingo PDF. Use color if possible, but B&W works too.
  • Gather Supplies: Pens, markers, or highlighters for people to mark squares.
  • Choose a Prize: A $5-20 gift card, candy basket, or even the "best parking spot for a week" at the office.
  • Print Extras: Have 2-3 extra cards in case people lose theirs.

During the Game

  • Set a Timer: 15 minutes is perfect. Announce '5 minutes left!' to create urgency.
  • Encourage Mingling: Announce "You have to talk to at least 5 different people!"
  • Play Music: Background holiday music sets the mood.
  • Join In: Organizers can play too!

4. For Players: How to Win & Have Fun

This isn't just about luck; there's a strategy! First, scan your board for squares you can get quickly. Yell out questions like "Who here is wearing red or green?" or "Who has a pet that isn't a dog or cat?" This gets multiple people to engage at once and helps you fill your board faster.

5. Zoom/Teams Edition: Virtual Party Guide

Playing remotely? No problem! Send each person one of the 8 bingo card pages beforehand. During the party, have the organizer call out questions related to the squares. Players can mark off their digital PDF or a printed copy at home. The first person to type "BINGO!" in the chat wins.

6. Game Variations

  • Team Mode: For large groups, split into teams. The first team to have one member get bingo wins.
  • Blackout: The ultimate challenge! The first person to fill their entire card wins a bigger prize.
  • Four Corners: The first person to get all four corner squares wins. A much faster version of the game.

7. Prize Ideas

The prize doesn't have to be expensive! The fun is in the winning. Good ideas include a Starbucks or Amazon gift card ($10-20), a nice box of chocolates, a bottle of wine, or a funny trophy that gets passed around each year.
