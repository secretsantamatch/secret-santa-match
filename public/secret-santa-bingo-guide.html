How to Play Secret Santa Bingo

The rules are incredibly simple. It's just like regular bingo, but instead of numbers, the squares are filled with common (and hilarious) gift exchange moments.

  1. Organize your gift exchange as you normally would. Use a free online generator to make it easy.
  2. Print the bingo cards. Download our free PDF (at the bottom of this post!) and give one card to each player. We've included 16 unique cards so everyone has a different board.
  3. Mark squares as gifts are opened. When a gift is opened, everyone checks their card. If they have a square that matches the event (e.g., "Someone gets socks"), they mark it off.
  4. Yell "BINGO!" The first person to get five squares in a row (horizontally, vertically, or diagonally) wins a prize!

Why This Game Works So Well

  • It Kills Awkward Silences: Everyone is actively participating and paying attention to every single gift.
  • It's an Instant Icebreaker: The funny squares ("The gift is obviously re-gifted") get people laughing and talking.
  • It's Super Easy: There's no complicated setup or rules to explain. Everyone gets it instantly.
  • It's Free! A great party game shouldn't cost you anything.

What's on the Bingo Cards?

We filled the squares with classic Secret Santa tropes. Here are a few of our favorites:

  • Someone gets a coffee mug
  • The gift is a candle
  • "Awww, you shouldn't have!"
  • The gift is alcohol
  • Someone has to guess their Santa
  • The wrapping paper is amazing
  • The gift is homemade

Pro Tips for the Host

  • Have a silly prize ready. The prize for winning bingo shouldn't be serious. Think a "World's Best Secret Santa" trophy, a bag of candy, or a funny pair of holiday socks.
  • Keep the game moving. Announce what's happening ("Okay, that's a coffee mug, check your cards!") to help everyone keep track.
  • Play for multiple winners. To keep the fun going, you can play for "first bingo," "second bingo," or even "blackout" (where someone has to fill their entire card).
