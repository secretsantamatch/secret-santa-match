What is a White Elephant Gift Exchange?

Unlike a traditional Secret Santa where you buy a gift for a specific person, in a White Elephant exchange, everyone brings one wrapped, anonymous gift. The gifts are placed in a central pile, and players take turns choosing and stealing gifts from each other. It's a game of luck, strategy, and good-natured betrayal.

The name comes from the idea of a "white elephant"‚Äîa gift that is extravagant but burdensome, something you'd want to get rid of. Today, it's all about bringing a funny, weird, or surprisingly cool gift within a set budget.

How to Play: The Official Rules

üéÅ The Setup

  1. Gather the Gifts: As guests arrive, have them place their single wrapped gift in a designated pile. No names should be on the gifts.
  2. Draw Numbers: Have each player draw a number from a hat. This number determines the order of play, from #1 to the total number of players.

‚ñ∂Ô∏è The Game Begins

  1. Player #1 Goes First: The person who drew #1 chooses any gift from the pile and opens it for everyone to see. Their turn is now over.
  2. Player #2's Turn: The second player has a choice: they can either steal Player #1's opened gift, or they can choose a new, unwrapped gift from the pile.
  3. The Stealing Chain:
    • If Player #2 steals Player #1's gift, Player #1 must then choose a new, unwrapped gift from the pile.
    • This continues for each player's turn. Any player can choose to either steal an already-opened gift or take a chance on a new one.
    • When a person's gift is stolen, they get a new turn to either steal from someone else or open a new gift.

üèÅ The End Game

  1. The game is over once the last player (#X) has taken their turn.
  2. The Final Swap (Optional but Recommended): Since Player #1 never got a chance to steal, many groups give them one final option to swap their gift with any other opened gift.

Popular Variations & House Rules

These are the rules that prevent total chaos and make the game more interesting.

  • The Three-Steal Rule (Highly Recommended): A single gift can only be stolen a maximum of three times. After the third steal, the gift is "frozen" or "dead" and belongs to the current owner for the rest of the game.
  • No Steal-Backs: You cannot immediately steal back the gift that was just stolen from you. You must wait at least one full turn.
  • Gift Themes: To guide the gift-giving, you can set a theme like "As Seen on TV," "Something for the Kitchen," or "Gag Gifts Only."

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between White Elephant and Secret Santa?

In Secret Santa, you buy a gift for a *specific person*. In White Elephant, you bring one gift for a *group game*. Secret Santa gifts are usually thoughtful and personal, while White Elephant gifts are often funny, strange, or useful to a general audience.

What are Yankee Swap and Dirty Santa?

They are just different names for the same game! "Yankee Swap" is common in the New England area of the U.S., while "Dirty Santa" is popular in the South. The "dirty" part refers to the act of stealing gifts, not the gifts themselves.

What's a good budget for a White Elephant exchange?

A budget of $20 to $25 is the sweet spot. It's low enough that no one gets too financially invested, but high enough to allow for creative, funny, and surprisingly good gifts.
